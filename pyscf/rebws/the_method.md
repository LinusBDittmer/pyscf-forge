# Retaining-the-Excitation Degree Perturbation Theory meets size-consistent Brillouin-Wigner perturbation theory

## Notation

We use the following notation:
- Unless explicitly stated otherwise, all equations are written in real spin orbitals. 
- Orbitals are indexed as i, j, k, ... if they are occupied, as a, b, c, ... if they are virtual (unoccupied) and as p, q, r, ... if they are either
- Spatial orbitals are dressed with an \overline if they refer to beta indices, e. g. p (alpha) and \overline{p} (beta)
- The letter used in expressions over spatial orbitals is irrelevant, i. e. a and \overline{a} are completely independent indices.
- Antisymmetrised electron repulsion integrals (ERIs) in Physicist notation are written as $$ \langle pq || rs \rangle $$
- Symmetric ERIs in Chemist notation are written as $$ (pq|rs) $$
- The extended Einstein sum convention is used: If an index appears on one side of the equation, but not the other, it is summed over

## General description of the method

The goal of this project is the implementation of a combined theory from RE perturbation theory and BWs perturbation theory.
We will limit ourselves to specifically second order. The central idea of this theory is a choice of partitioning that
is given by:
$$ \hat{H}^{(0)} = \hat{H}^{(0)}_{MP} + \alpha \hat{H}^{(0)}_{BWs} + \beta \hat{H}^{(0)}_{\Delta RE} $$
In this equation, alpha and beta are real scaling parameters. 

### The correlation energy

At second order, the correlation energy is given by the
standard equation in spin orbitals:
$$ E_{corr} = 0.25 \langle ij || ab \rangle t^{ab}_{ij} $$
where $$ t^{ab}_{ij} $$ are the first order doubles amplitudes. In restricted spatial orbitals, the energy is given by:
$$ E_{corr} = (ia|jb) (2 t^{ab}_{ij} - t^{ab}_{ji}) $$
In unrestricted spatial orbitals, the energy is given by:
$$ E_{corr} = (ia|jb) (t^{ab}_{ij} - t^{ab}_{ji}) + (ia|\overline{jb}) t^{a\overline{b}}_{i\overline{j}} $$

### The t-amplitudes
The t-amplitudes are generated by solving the amplitude equation, which will be discussed later. Since the amplitude equation
in RE-BWs2 are neither linear nor diagonalisable, they have to be solved for iteratively, exactly like in coupled-cluster codes.
We will discuss the algorithm in the unrestricted case, from which the restricted case follows straightfowardly:
1. The initial guess are the MP2 amplitudes
2. In each iteration, the residuals $$ R^{ab}_{ij} $$ (alpha-alpha), $$ R^{a\overline{b}}_{i\overline{j}} $$ (alpha-beta) and $$ R^{\overline{a}\overline{b}}_{\overline{i}\overline{j}} $$ (beta-beta) are computed.
3. The residual is normalised by dividing it by (e_a + e_b - e_i - e_j) where e is an orbital energy. Mind the spin blocks in this step.
4. A new amplitude is generated by subtracting the normalised residual from the current amplitude guess.
5. After a set number of iterations (default: 3), direct inversion of the iterative subspace is used to generate the amplitude instead.
6. Once the magnitude per element of the residual falls below a certain convergence criterion, the amplitudes are converged and the loop is exited.
